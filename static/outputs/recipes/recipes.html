Lasagne

White Sauce
melt butter whisk in flour → paste stir in milk nutmeg + salt
Layers
pasta sheets bolognaise white sauce mozzarella slices → repeat top grated cheddar bake 180°C ~30min
Ingredients · Serves 6
  • 500g beef mince
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 onions, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp each: salt, pepper, herbs de Provence, sugar
  • 1 × 400g tin chopped tomatoes
  • 70g butter
  • 3–4 tbsp flour
  • 750ml milk
  • Pinch of nutmeg
  • Lasagne sheets
  • Mozzarella, sliced
  • Grated cheddar
Full Recipe · Serves 6
  1. Bolognaise: fry 500g mince in 2 tbsp olive oil. Add 3 diced onions and 3 minced garlic cloves. Add 1 tsp each of salt, pepper, herbs de Provence, and sugar. Add 1 can chopped tomatoes. Simmer until reduced.
  2. White Sauce: melt 70g butter in a pan. Add 3–4 tbsp flour and whisk into a paste. Gradually stir in 750ml milk, a little at a time, stirring constantly until smooth and thick. Season with a pinch of nutmeg and salt.
  3. Layer up:
    1. Lasagne sheets
    2. Bolognaise
    3. White sauce
    4. Mozzarella slices
    5. Repeat until dish is full
    6. Finish with grated cheddar on top
  4. Bake at 180°C for approximately 30 minutes until golden and bubbling.

Risotto

tldr
sauté onion garlic toast arborio add white wine ladle warm stock → stir 18–20min finish cold butter + parmesan
Ingredients · Serves 4
  • 1–1.2L chicken or vegetable stock
  • 1 onion, finely diced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 300g arborio rice
  • 1 large glass dry white wine
  • 60g cold butter, diced
  • 80g parmesan, grated
  • Salt and pepper
Full Recipe · Serves 4
  1. Keep 1–1.2L chicken or vegetable stock warm in a pot on low heat.
  2. Sauté 1 finely diced onion in 2 tbsp olive oil until soft and translucent, about 5min. Add 2 garlic cloves, 1min more.
  3. Add 300g arborio rice, stir to coat in oil. Toast for 2min.
  4. Pour in a large glass of dry white wine. Stir until absorbed.
  5. Add stock one ladle at a time, stirring constantly and waiting for each ladle to absorb before adding the next. Continue 18–20min until rice is al dente.
  6. Remove from heat. Stir in 60g cold diced butter and 80g grated parmesan. Season generously. Rest 2min before serving.
  7. Works as a base — add sautéed mushrooms, peas + pancetta, or roasted squash + sage.

Carbonara

tldr
fry guanciale whisk egg yolks + pecorino + pepper cook pasta al dente reserve pasta water combine off heat toss + loosen with water
Ingredients · Serves 4
  • 150g guanciale (or pancetta)
  • 4 egg yolks + 1 whole egg
  • 80g pecorino romano, finely grated
  • Black pepper
  • 400g spaghetti or rigatoni
Full Recipe · Serves 4 · No cream
  1. Dice 150g guanciale (or pancetta). Cook in a dry pan over medium heat until crisp and fat has rendered. Keep fat in the pan, take off heat.
  2. Whisk together 4 egg yolks + 1 whole egg, 80g finely grated pecorino romano, generous black pepper.
  3. Cook 400g spaghetti or rigatoni in well-salted water until al dente. Reserve a large mug of pasta water before draining.
  4. Add drained pasta to the pan with guanciale (off heat). Toss to coat in the fat.
  5. Add the egg and cheese mixture, tossing quickly. Add pasta water a splash at a time — the residual heat creates a creamy sauce. Don't put it back on the flame or it scrambles.
  6. Serve immediately with more pecorino and cracked black pepper.

Dahl

tldr
fry onion garlic ginger add spices add red lentils add tinned toms + coconut milk + stock simmer 25min lemon + coriander
Ingredients · Serves 4
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • Oil
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • ½ tsp chilli flakes
  • 250g red lentils, rinsed
  • 1 × 400g tin chopped tomatoes
  • 400ml coconut milk
  • 300ml vegetable stock
  • Salt, lemon juice, fresh coriander, yoghurt to serve
Full Recipe · Serves 4
  1. Sauté 1 large diced onion in oil until golden, about 8–10min. Add 3 garlic cloves and 1 tbsp grated ginger, cook 2min.
  2. Add 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp turmeric, 1 tsp garam masala, ½ tsp chilli flakes. Stir and fry 1min.
  3. Add 250g rinsed red lentils, 1 × 400g tin chopped tomatoes, 400ml coconut milk, 300ml vegetable stock.
  4. Bring to a boil, then simmer uncovered 20–25min, stirring occasionally, until lentils are completely soft and thick.
  5. Season with salt and a squeeze of lemon juice. Adjust spice to taste.
  6. Serve with fresh coriander, a dollop of yoghurt, and rice or flatbread.

Rosiecurry

tldr
fry onion + garlic add cumin + chilli add chickpeas + veg add chopped toms + garam masala + turmeric → simmer 20min stir in cream + season
Ingredients · Serves 4
  • 2 onions, diced
  • Oil
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp chilli (to taste)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 × 400g tins chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • Veg of choice (peppers, spinach, courgette)
  • 1 × 400g tin chopped tomatoes
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • 100ml single cream or coconut cream
Full Recipe · Serves 4
  1. Sauté 2 diced onions in oil over medium heat until golden, about 8–10min. Add 4 minced garlic cloves, cook 2min.
  2. Add 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp chilli (to taste), and 1 tsp salt. Stir and fry 1min until fragrant.
  3. Add 2 × 400g tins of chickpeas (drained and rinsed). Add any other veg you have — peppers, spinach, courgette all work well.
  4. Pour in 1 × 400g tin chopped tomatoes. Add 1 tsp garam masala and ½ tsp turmeric. Stir well, bring to a simmer, and cook uncovered for 20min.
  5. Stir in 100ml single cream or coconut cream. Taste and season with more salt if needed.
Variations
  • All amounts are suggestions — this recipe is versatile.
  • Protein: any meat works — add in step 3 and cook until sealed before the tomatoes go in. Tofu or paneer work too — fry separately until golden and stir in at the end.
  • Veg timing: firm fresh veg (peppers, courgette, cauliflower) — fry with the onion or add before the tomatoes so they soften. Leafy veg (spinach, kale) — stir in at the very end, they just need to wilt. Already roasted veg — also add at the end, they just need warming through.
  • Chickpeas: one tin makes a saucier curry, two is more substantial. Red lentils (250g, rinsed) are a good swap and will break down into the sauce.
  • Spices: all amounts are vague suggestions.
  • Cream: coconut cream gives a richer, slightly sweet sauce; single cream is more neutral. A dollop of yoghurt stirred in off the heat if it's too spicy. Can also skip entirely.
  • Toppings: fresh coriander; parsley. Crumbled feta.

Hash

tldr
parboil potato fry onion + pepper add chorizo add potato → crisp make wells → crack eggs cover → cook until set
Ingredients · Serves 2–3
  • 400g potatoes, diced (2cm cubes)
  • Oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 red pepper, diced
  • 100g chorizo or sausage, sliced
  • 3 eggs
  • Salt and pepper
  • Parsley, hot sauce, or sour cream to serve
Full Recipe · Serves 2–3
  1. Parboil 400g diced potato (2cm cubes) for 5min until just tender. Drain well.
  2. Heat oil in a large oven-safe frying pan. Sauté 1 diced onion and 1 diced red pepper until soft, about 6min.
  3. Add 100g sliced chorizo or diced sausage. Fry until crisp.
  4. Add parboiled potato. Press down, don't stir — let it crisp 3–4min. Flip sections and crisp the other side. Season well.
  5. Make 3 wells in the hash. Crack an egg into each.
  6. Cover with a lid and cook on low heat 4–6min until whites are set but yolks still runny. Or finish in a 200°C oven for 5min.
  7. Top with parsley, hot sauce, or sour cream.

Moussaka

tldr
salt + drain eggplant fry eggplant brown lamb + onion garlic add cinnamon + allspice + tomato paste + tinned toms layer eggplant → meat → repeat make béchamel + egg + parmesan pour over top bake 180°C ~45min
Ingredients · Serves 4–6
  • 2 large eggplants
  • Olive oil
  • 450g ground lamb or beef
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp cinnamon, 1 tsp allspice
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1 × 400g tin diced tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp butter, 2 tbsp flour, 500ml milk (béchamel)
  • ¼ cup parmesan, grated
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • Pinch of nutmeg
Full Recipe · Serves 4–6
  1. Slice 2 large eggplants into rounds. Salt generously, leave 20–30min, then pat dry. Fry in olive oil in batches until golden on both sides. Set aside.
  2. Brown 450g ground lamb (or beef) in a pan. Add 1 finely chopped onion and 2 minced garlic cloves, cook until soft.
  3. Stir in 1 tbsp tomato paste, 1 tsp cinnamon, 1 tsp allspice, salt and pepper. Add 1 × 400g tin diced tomatoes. Simmer 15min until reduced.
  4. Béchamel: melt 2 tbsp butter, stir in 2 tbsp flour, cook 1min. Gradually whisk in 500ml milk until smooth and thick. Remove from heat, stir in ¼ cup grated parmesan, 1 beaten egg, and a pinch of nutmeg.
  5. Layer in a baking dish: eggplant → meat sauce → eggplant → meat sauce. Pour the béchamel over the top and spread evenly.
  6. Bake at 180°C for ~45min until the top is set and golden. Rest 10min before serving.

Barley Risotto

tldr
warm beef broth sauté shallot + garlic in butter fry mushrooms + thyme add barley → toast 1min add wine → absorb ladle broth → stir until absorbed → repeat finish parmesan
Ingredients · Serves 6
  • 4 cups (≈950ml) beef broth, low sodium
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 1 shallot, minced
  • 2 garlic cloves, sliced
  • 225g (½ lb) fresh mushrooms
  • ¼ tsp fresh thyme
  • 1 cup pearl barley
  • ½ cup dry white wine
  • ¼ cup parmesan, grated
  • Salt and pepper
Full Recipe · Serves 6 · seasonsandsuppers.ca
  1. Heat 4 cups beef broth (low sodium) in a saucepan and keep warm on low.
  2. In a large deep skillet, melt 2 tbsp butter over medium heat. Add 1 minced shallot and 2 sliced garlic cloves, cook until softened.
  3. Add ½ lb (225g) fresh mushrooms and ¼ tsp fresh thyme. Season with salt and pepper. Cook 5–6min until mushrooms are golden.
  4. Add 1 cup pearl barley and 1 tbsp butter. Stir for 1min. Pour in ½ cup dry white wine, cook until absorbed (3–4min).
  5. Add broth one ladle at a time, stirring and waiting for each ladle to absorb before adding the next. Continue until barley is al dente — about 30–35min total. Add hot water if you run out of broth.
  6. Stir in ¼ cup grated parmesan. Season to taste. Serve garnished with fresh thyme and extra parmesan.

Chilli Con Carne

tldr
brown mince fry onion garlic add cumin, smoked paprika, chilli, oregano add tomato paste add tinned toms + kidney beans + stock simmer 30min dark chocolate + season
Ingredients · Serves 4
  • 500g beef mince
  • Oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1–2 tsp chilli powder, 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 × 400g tin chopped tomatoes
  • 1 × 400g tin kidney beans, drained
  • 200ml beef stock
  • Small square dark chocolate
  • Pinch of cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper
  • Rice, sour cream, cheddar, jalapeños to serve
Full Recipe · Serves 4
  1. Brown 500g beef mince in a hot pan with a little oil. Season, then remove and set aside.
  2. In the same pan, sauté 1 large diced onion until soft. Add 3 minced garlic cloves, cook 1min.
  3. Add 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1–2 tsp chilli powder (to taste), 1 tsp dried oregano. Stir and fry 1min.
  4. Add 1 tbsp tomato paste, cook 1min. Return the mince.
  5. Pour in 1 × 400g tin chopped tomatoes, 1 × 400g tin kidney beans (drained), and 200ml beef stock. Stir well.
  6. Simmer uncovered on low heat for 30–40min, stirring occasionally, until thick and rich.
  7. Stir in a small square of dark chocolate and a pinch of cinnamon. Season well with salt and pepper.
  8. Serve with rice, sour cream, grated cheddar, and jalapeños.

Oven Cauliflower

tldr
boil whole cauli 5–7min rub oil + cumin + coriander roast 200°C 30–40min whisk tahini + yoghurt + lemon + garlic pour over
Ingredients · Serves 2–4
  • 1 whole cauliflower
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp ground cumin, 1 tsp ground coriander
  • Salt and pepper
  • 4 tbsp tahini
  • 150g Greek yoghurt
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • Flat-leaf parsley (optional)
Full Recipe · Serves 2–4 · Ottolenghi
  1. Preheat oven to 200°C.
  2. Whole: trim outer leaves and cut stem flat. Boil in well-salted water 5–7min until slightly tender. Drain well. Wedges/florets: skip the boil, just cut up.
  3. Mix 3 tbsp olive oil, 1 tsp ground cumin, 1 tsp ground coriander, salt and pepper. Rub all over the cauliflower.
  4. Roast on a baking tray: 30–40min for whole, 20–30min for wedges/florets, until golden and crispy.
  5. Tahini-yoghurt sauce: whisk 4 tbsp tahini, 150g Greek yoghurt, juice of 1 lemon, 1 minced garlic clove, salt and pepper. Add a splash of water to thin until pourable — tahini seizes when yoghurt is added, just keep adding water.
  6. Pour sauce over or serve alongside for dipping. Top with flat-leaf parsley if using.

Ingredients · Serves 4
  • 200g block feta
  • 500g cherry tomatoes
  • 4 garlic cloves, whole

  • Chilli flakes
  • Olive oil
  • Black pepper
  • 400g penne or rigatoni
  • Fresh basil to finish
Full Recipe · Serves 4

Finnische Lachssuppe

tldr

Ingredients · Serves 4
  • 3 leeks, thinly sliced
  • 3 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 400g waxy potatoes, diced
  • 500g salmon fillet, cut in chunks
  • Butter or olive oil
  • 1.2L fish stock
  • Salt and pepper
  • 200ml cream
  • Fresh dill
  • Lemon
Full Recipe · Serves 4 · lacuisinedegeraldine.fr
  1. Slice 3 leeks thinly and rinse well. Peel and slice 3 carrots. Dice 400g waxy potatoes. Cut 500g salmon fillet into large chunks.

  • 250g fresh mozzarella
  • Fresh mint
  • Lemon zest and juice
  • Olive oil
  • Flaky salt and pepper
  • Prosciutto (optional)
Full Recipe · Serves 4
  1. Blanch 300g peas (fresh or frozen) in boiling salted water for 1–2min. Drain and refresh in cold water. Pat dry.
  2. Tear 250g fresh mozzarella into pieces on a platter.
  3. Scatter the peas over and around the mozzarella.
  4. Add fresh mint leaves and lemon zest. Dress with olive oil, lemon juice, flaky salt and pepper.
  5. Optional: prosciutto draped over works well if not keeping it vegetarian.

Beans & Parmy

tldr
drain + rinse beans toss lemon + oil + garlic add herbs shave parmesan on top flaky salt + more oil
Ingredients · Serves 4
  • 2 × 400g tins cannellini or butter beans
  • 4 tbsp good olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 garlic cloves, finely grated
  • Salt and black pepper
  • Fresh flat-leaf parsley
  • Sage leaves (optional, fried in butter)
  • Parmesan for shaving
Full Recipe · Serves 4
  1. Drain and rinse 2 × 400g tins of cannellini or butter beans. Pat dry.
  2. Toss with 4 tbsp good olive oil, juice of 1 lemon, 2 very finely grated garlic cloves, salt, and black pepper.
  3. Add fresh herbs: flat-leaf parsley, and optionally sage leaves fried in butter until crisp.
  4. Transfer to a plate. Use a peeler to shave generous ribbons of parmesan over the top.
  5. Finish with extra olive oil and flaky salt. Great alongside roasted veg or grilled meat.

White Choc Cheesecake

tldr
crush biscuits + melt butter → press into tin melt white choc mix cream cheese + crème double + mascarpone stir in chocolate fold in blueberries spread over base chill 3hrs+
Ingredients · 20cm springform tin
  • 200g digestive or whole grain biscuits
  • 100g butter, melted
  • 400g good-quality white chocolate
  • 250g cream cheese
  • 250ml crème double
  • 250g mascarpone
  • 250g blueberries
Full Recipe · 20cm springform tin · zuckerzimtundliebe.de
  1. Crush 200g whole grain biscuits (e.g. Landkekse/digestives) into fine crumbs. Mix with 100g melted butter. Press firmly into the base of a 20cm springform tin. Refrigerate.
  2. Melt 400g good-quality white chocolate in a bowl over a pot of barely simmering water — bowl must not touch the water and no steam or water must get in. Melt slowly on low heat. Set aside to cool slightly.
  3. Beat together 250g cream cheese, 250ml crème double, and 250g mascarpone with a wooden spoon until smooth.
  4. Stir the melted chocolate into the cream cheese mixture quickly. It may look like it won't come together at first — keep stirring and it will.
  5. Fold in most of the 250g blueberries, reserving a handful for the top.
  6. Spread the filling over the biscuit base and smooth the surface. Refrigerate for at least 3 hours (overnight is better).
  7. Remove from the tin, top with the reserved blueberries, and serve promptly.
